This document is a basic knowledge document about options marketplaces and derivatives trading. 

Terminology:
	1. Bid: The price (max/highest) for which someone is willing to buy something. 
			Example: expected price tomorrow = $100, bid $60, $100 cushion. 

	2. Offer: The price at which someone is willing to sell something.
			  Example:  expected price tomorrow = $100, offer selling price $170, $70 cushion.

	3. Size: The amount of contracts one is willing to trade at a price. 
			 Example: expected price tomorrow = $100, at bid of $60 size of 4x means trade 4 times (4 contracts), i.e. 4 contracts * $100 cushion.

	4. Make a market: To provide a bid and ask price for the market as well as bid size & ask size. 
					  Example: $60 bid for 4 (willing to buy 4 contracts at $60)
					  		   10 at $170 (willing to sell 10 contracts at $170)

	5. Spread: The difference between the bid and ask price. 
			   Example: bid = $60, offer = $170, spread (bid - offer) = $110.

	6. Hedge: A trade or investment to reduce the risk of price movement in as asset. 
			  Example: $1000 bet - Real Madrid to win tonight [exposure on that's positively correlated to RMs performance]
					   3 different hedge that reduce the risk of first bet:
					   a. RM will score < 120 points tonight
					   b. Score 40-40 at half, sell them winning at 55%
					   c. Shoot < 30% from 3 point range 

	7. Paper: The interested parties trading against market makers.

	8. Broker: A person or company that acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers.

	9. Tick Size: The smallest increment between one price and next highest or lowest price.

	10. Queue Priority: The way electronic exchange determine who is first in line to do a trade. A common method that is used to 					  determine who's first in the line is the price time priority method.
